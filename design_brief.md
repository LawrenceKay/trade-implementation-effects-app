# Design Brief — Trade Implementation Effects App

## 1. Purpose

Help investment analysts, government officials, and trade specialists assess the institutional quality of countries as long-term investment destinations, by measuring the gap between the depth of a country's trade commitments and its actual record of implementing them.

---

## 2. The Problem

Investors and trade specialists face a persistent information problem: a country may have signed dozens of trade agreements, but the mere existence of agreements tells you little about whether they work. What matters for investment is whether a country's institutions are capable of honouring commitments over time — reducing regulatory friction, settling disputes predictably, and keeping trade policy stable.

Existing tools answer either the commitment question (how deep are this country's agreements?) or the friction question (how many disputes or NTMs does it have?) in isolation. No tool yet maps the *gap* between the two — and it is precisely that gap that reveals institutional quality.

---

## 3. Core Analytical Framework

The app measures **institutional quality as the distance between commitment and implementation** across a set of countries and measures.

### Dimension A — Commitment depth and breadth
What has the country formally signed up to? This is a measure of the ambition and scope of its trade agreements.

| Measure | Source | What it captures |
|---------|--------|-----------------|
| Number of trade agreements in force | World Bank DTA 2.0 | Breadth of engagement |
| Average DTA depth score | World Bank DTA 2.0 | Depth of provisions across 18 policy areas |
| PTA design score | DESTA | Institutional design quality (dispute mechanisms, review clauses, etc.) |
| Tariff liberalisation commitments | WITS / WTO TAO | Share of tariff lines bound and applied rates |

### Dimension B — Implementation and use
How well has the country actually delivered on its commitments? This is a measure of institutional follow-through.

| Measure | Source | What it captures |
|---------|--------|-----------------|
| WTO dispute rate (as respondent) | WTO DSB | Frequency of being challenged for non-compliance |
| Investor-state dispute rate | UNCTAD ISDS Navigator | Frequency of investor challenges — a direct investment climate signal |
| Trade policy distortions | Global Trade Alert | Count of distorting interventions relative to liberalising ones |
| NTM coverage ratio | UNCTAD NTM / TRAINS | Share of trade subject to non-tariff measures |
| Specific Trade Concerns filed against | WTO ePing STCs | Regulatory friction raised by trading partners |
| Ad valorem equivalent of NTMs | World Bank WITS AVE | Tariff-equivalent cost of non-tariff barriers |

### The Gap Score
For each country, the app computes a **Gap Score**: the normalised distance between its Commitment Score (Dimension A) and its Implementation Score (Dimension B). A high Gap Score signals that a country has made ambitious trade commitments it has not reliably honoured — a risk signal for long-term investors. A low Gap Score signals institutional consistency.

```
Gap Score = Commitment Score − Implementation Score
             (both normalised to 0–100)
```

Countries are segmented into four quadrants:

| Quadrant | Commitment | Implementation | Investment signal |
|----------|-----------|----------------|------------------|
| Strong performers | High | High | Low risk — stable, open investment climate |
| Over-committed | High | Low | High risk — ambitious agreements, poor follow-through |
| Quiet compliers | Low | High | Medium risk — limited agreements but reliable within them |
| Disengaged | Low | Low | High risk — shallow integration and poor compliance |

---

## 4. User Experience

### Entry point — the map
The user lands on a world map (Folium choropleth). Countries are shaded by their Gap Score — a diverging colour scale from green (low gap, strong institutional quality) to red (high gap, weak follow-through).

### Hover
Hovering over a country shows a tooltip with:
- Country name
- Overall Gap Score
- Commitment Score
- Implementation Score
- One-line interpretation (e.g. "High commitments, poor follow-through — elevated investment risk")

### Click / drill-down
Clicking a country opens a side panel with:
- A radar chart showing the country's scores across all individual measures
- A bar chart comparing the country against a user-selected peer group
- A summary narrative generated by Claude, grounded in the data
- Links to source datasets for due diligence

### Controls
- Filter by region (e.g. Southeast Asia, Sub-Saharan Africa)
- Filter by agreement type (bilateral, regional, WTO-plus)
- Select which measures to include in the Gap Score
- Compare up to three countries side by side

## 5. Outputs

| Output | Format | Purpose |
|--------|--------|---------|
| Choropleth map | Interactive Folium map | Entry point — visual scan of institutional quality by country |
| Country scorecard | Radar + bar charts | Drill-down into individual measure performance |
| Gap Score table | Sortable Streamlit dataframe | Ranked list of all countries by Gap Score |
| Country narrative | Claude-generated text | Plain-English interpretation of a country's record |
| Export | CSV / PDF | Download scores and narratives for offline use |

---

## 6. Data and Coverage

Initial build will cover the **top 60 countries by FDI inflows** (World Bank / UNCTAD data), which captures the most investment-relevant markets while keeping the data pipeline manageable. Coverage will expand in later versions.

Data will be sourced, cleaned, and stored in the app's knowledge base. All scores will be recomputable from raw sources — no black-box indices.

---

## 7. Constraints and Limitations

| Constraint | Detail |
|------------|--------|
| Data recency | DTA 2.0 and DESTA are updated periodically, not in real time. Scores reflect the latest available vintage. |
| NTM data gaps | NTM coverage data is incomplete for lower-income countries. Where data is missing, this is flagged explicitly in the UI. |
| Gap Score is relative | The Gap Score ranks countries against each other, not against an absolute standard. A low score means better than peers, not perfect. |
| AI narratives | Claude-generated country summaries are clearly labelled as AI-generated and must not be treated as verified analysis without human review. |
| Composite index limitations | Aggregating across measures involves weighting choices. Default weights are equal; users can adjust them. The methodology is fully documented. |

---

## 8. Success Criteria

The app succeeds if a user can, within two minutes of landing on it:

1. Identify which countries present the strongest institutional risk for trade-linked investment
2. Understand *why* a given country scores as it does — which specific measures drive its Gap Score
3. Compare a shortlist of countries on the measures most relevant to their investment thesis
